Diversifying the world's sources of renewable energy.

At Motus, we're using piezoelectric materials to harness the energy released from earthquakes and converting that into electrical energy to power the processes needed to rebuild the affected area.

Rebuilding Nations

Earthquakes have devestating impacts--killing lives and destroying homes while releasing a huge amount of energy. The reparation costs and time it takes to rebuild an economy following large earthquakes are also massive. The enormous enviornmental impact that earthquakes have on the affected area is not discussed enough. Developing countries who go through earthquakes tend to rely heavily on fossil fuels such as coal to power the machinery and processes associated with rebuilding their country to where it was pre-earthquake. Energy prices often skyrocket during this period of time as well. We're here to alleviate this.

Introducing Piezoelectricity

Piezoelectricity is electric energy that can be collected from piezoelectric materials. When stress (in the form of vibrations) is applied on these materials, it creates a dipole (or separation of charge) that ultimately creates a voltage which can be harnessed as electrical energy. At Motus, we want to build a piezoelectric energy harvester that can be mounted on the ground in areas where earthquakes are frequent and the vibrations are noticeable. This system would allow us to harness the vibrational energy from earthquakes, convert it into electrical energy, and then stored in an energy storage device to power the activities associated with fixing the damages caused by earthquakes. Our piezoelectric system will be comprised of 4 parts: The Excitation base, Piezolayer, Substructure, and the Mass.

Excitation Base

The vibrations from the earthquake will enter the excitation base at all angles and the kinetic energy of these vibrations then get transferred to the piezo layer.

Piezolayer

Allows the encaptured vibrations to be transformed into electrical energy. We plan on using PZT as our piezomaterial because it's proven to be the most successful at absorbing high frequency vibrations.

Substructure

Prevents our piezoelectric system from being destroyed during an actual earthquake. The arcs in the machinery which will be made out of metal with withstand the pressure from the earthquakes as well as conduct the newly transformed electrical energy from the piezolayer.

Mass

The last stop. This is the intermediary point between when the energy is transformed and its eventual distribution across the region. We imagine this to be an energy storage plant.
